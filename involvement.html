What Can We Do?

Speak up ~ You can spread awareness of the critical conditions many people are experiencing without the support they deserve. Across the country and the world, individuals from marginalized communities face barriers to healthcare due to systemic inequality, racism, poverty, and a lack of culturally competent care. By using your voice whether through social media, conversations, school projects, or community events you help shine a light on the struggles that are too often ignored. Advocacy begins with awareness, and awareness begins with people like you who care enough to speak up. Don’t underestimate the power of your words. One voice can spark a movement.

Volunteer, taking action starts in your own community. You can volunteer with local organizations dedicated to promoting health equity and supporting underserved populations. Whether you're helping patients check in at a community health clinic, distributing resources through the American Red Cross, or raising awareness at local events, your time and effort make a real difference. Volunteering not only helps othersit also educates you about the realities of health disparities and how change begins on the ground. To find opportunities near you, explore local health centers, non-profits, or public health departments. Every helping hand brings us closer to a more just and equitable healthcare system.

Donations, no matter how small, can make a significant impact. We've partnered with a few great organizations that are working to reduce health disparities:

  • Amy's GoFundMe
  • Gage’s GoFundMe
  • Michael’s Family GoFundMe

Helping out by donating a little money can make a drastic difference in a child's life. All our donations are given to local clinics, and we are excited to give everyone an opportunity to help those who are suffering.
